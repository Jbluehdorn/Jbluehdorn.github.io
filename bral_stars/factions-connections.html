Factions & Connections

Factions & Connections

This page summarizes the major factions and key figures on the Rock of Bral. When creating your character, you can optionally establish a connection to any of these — a past employer, a debt, a rival, a patron, or a friend.

You don't need a connection, but having one gives the DM more hooks to work with.

The Seekers

Adventurers' Guild — friendly to players

The Seekers are the informal adventurers' guild of the Rock. They maintain the Adventurers' Board at the The Laughing Beholder and provide a loose support network for people who do dangerous work for money.

Connection ideas: You're a registered member. You owe them a favor. They vouched for you when you first arrived. A Seeker died on a job with you.

The Laughing Beholder & Large Luigi

Neutral ground — friendly to all

Large Luigi is the beholder proprietor of the The Laughing Beholder — the social hub of the Rock. He's neutral, good-humored, and terrifyingly well-connected. He knows everyone, remembers every debt, and doesn't pick sides.

Connection ideas: You're a regular and he knows your drink. You owe him money. He gave you a tip that saved your life. He once disintegrated someone on your behalf and you've never asked why.

Prince Andru & the Crown

The ruling power — politically neutral to cautious

Left to right: Proconsul Gadaric Main · Lord Diadan Cartan · Prince Andru · Lady Cerena · Captain Mahaxara Khal

Prince Andru rules the Rock from The Palace of Bral. He's pragmatic and not cruel, but he is absolutely self-interested. His inner circle includes his Proconsul, key noble advisors, and Captain Mahaxara Khal, head of the Royal Guard.

Connection ideas: You once did a job the crown needed deniability for. You have a contact in the palace staff. Andru owes you a small favor — or you owe him one. The Royal Guard has its eye on you for something you may or may not have done.

House Traken

Merchant noble house — transactional

House Traken is the dominant merchant family on the Rock. They deal in trade, contracts, and information. They hire adventurers regularly for escort, retrieval, and "competitive intelligence" work.

Connection ideas: You completed a job for them and they paid well. You failed a job for them and the debt is outstanding. Lady Sera Traken has taken an interest in your skills. You stumbled onto something House Traken would rather keep quiet.

The Juggler's Guild

Thieves' guild — dangerous, transactional

The Juggler's Guild controls the criminal underworld of the Low City. The identity of their leader — the Juggler — is unknown. They deal in smuggling, information, fencing, and enforcement.

Connection ideas: You owe them money. You used to work for them. You know something about the Juggler's identity. They left you alone as long as you stayed out of their business — so far.

Careful

Characters with ties to the Juggler's Guild will be viewed with suspicion by the Crown and the Seekers. That's not necessarily a problem — just something to know.

The Arcane

Spelljamming helm traders — strictly neutral

The Arcane are the blue-skinned traders who hold a near-monopoly on spelljamming helms. They sell to anyone and take sides with no one. Their Arcane Factor on the Rock maintains a small office near The Docks.

Connection ideas: You have an open account with the Factor — useful for helm repairs. You're investigating where their helms actually come from. The Factor approached you about a "retrieval" job they can't be seen doing themselves.

The Elven Imperial Fleet

Distant power — occasionally present

The elves maintain a presence in Realmspace through their Imperial Fleet, crewed primarily by Astral Elves who have lived in Wildspace for centuries. They patrol trade routes, hunt slavers, and occasionally take an interest in Rock politics.

Connection ideas: You served as a hired hand aboard an elven vessel. An elven officer saved your life and you've never been able to repay the debt. The Fleet is watching you for reasons you don't fully understand.

The Giff

Mercenary specialists — hire-able

Giff are hippo-folk mercenaries with an obsessive love of firearms, discipline, and military hierarchy. A Giff platoon is available for hire on the Rock, and individual Giff sometimes work as independent contractors.

Connection ideas: You served alongside a Giff unit. A Giff officer trained you in firearms or tactics. You won a bet against a Giff in a shooting contest and they've never forgiven you.

The Neogi

Slavers — enemy faction

Neogi are spider-like slavers who operate throughout Wildspace in their Deathspider ships. They are not present on the Rock — they're not welcome — but their shadow reaches everywhere there's a slave trade.

Connection ideas: They took someone you care about. You escaped from one of their ships. You've been hunting a specific neogi captain. You have information about their operations that multiple factions would pay for.
